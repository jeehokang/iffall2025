Project Title: Toilet Paper Orientation Microsite

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Toilet_paper_orientation

Present the famous “over vs. under” debate through history, arguments, public opinions, and cultural meaning.

  • Page 1: Home / Overview - Introduce the concept of toilet paper orientation (“over” vs. “under”) and why it became a familiar debate. Define both orientations, show simple comparison visuals, and explain why people care (aesthetics, hygiene, convenience).
  • Page 2: History - Describe the invention of perforated toilet paper and the 1891 patent showing the “over” orientation. Highlight the 1977 Ann Landers column that sparked nationwide discussion and later media references that kept the topic alive.
  • Page 3: The Debate - Present both sides' main arguments.
    • Over: easier to see and grab, cleaner, allows decorative fold, shows branding.
    • Under: tidier appearance, safer around pets or toddlers, prevents unrolling in RVs.
    Mention other dispenser styles that bypass the issue.
  • Page 4: Public Opinion & Culture - Summarize surveys (around 70% prefer “over”), behavioral habits, and sociological insights into everyday routines.
  • Page 5: Sources - Cite the original Wikipedia article and additional references such as patents, surveys, and cultural mentions. Include image or illustration credits.
